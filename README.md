## Project Highlights

### 🏢 Qlyntix Technologies — ISO/IEC 27001:2022 ISMS Implementation

**Context:** Qlyntix Technologies is a cloud-native SaaS company delivering a CRM platform to global enterprise clients, handling sensitive customer data, financial records, employee information, and proprietary source code. The organisation faced active cybersecurity incidents — targeted phishing campaigns, suspicious cloud authentication events, and security gaps identified during client-led assessments — exposing critical weaknesses in access governance, third-party risk oversight, and a complete absence of formal risk management. These gaps posed direct threats to client retention, regulatory standing, and competitive positioning in the enterprise market.

**Engagement:** Led the end-to-end design and implementation of an ISO/IEC 27001:2022-aligned ISMS across seven structured phases:

- **Phase 1 — Business Case & Governance:** Built a business case anchored in incident data and financial exposure analysis, securing CEO sponsorship, formal budget allocation, and a cross-functional ISMS governance committee across HR, Finance, IT, DevOps, Marketing, and Procurement
- **Phase 2 — Organisational Context & Scope:** Conducted stakeholder analysis, SWOT and PESTLE assessments, and defined ISMS scope across cloud infrastructure, CI/CD pipelines, corporate IT, and vendor integrations
- **Phase 3 — Risk Assessment & Treatment:** Developed risk register, likelihood/impact scoring model, asset classification (Restricted → Public), and a prioritised risk treatment plan targeting unauthenticated cloud admin access, phishing susceptibility, and unsecured vendor integrations
- **Phase 4 — Statement of Applicability:** Mapped all 93 Annex A controls to identified risks with documented justifications, applicability decisions, and linkage to implemented policies and procedures
- **Phase 5 — Controls Implementation:** MFA enforcement across cloud admin accounts, RBAC and IAM audits, endpoint protection, SIEM-based log monitoring, secure SDLC practices, phishing simulation programs, and a vendor risk assessment lifecycle
- **Phase 6 — Performance Evaluation:** KPI framework covering phishing click-rate reduction, patch compliance rates, training completion; internal audit programs and management review cycles
- **Phase 7 — Certification Readiness:** Stage 1 (documentation review) and Stage 2 (implementation audit) preparation, ensuring audit-trail completeness across incident logs, access reviews, vulnerability scan records, and corrective action registers

**Outcome:** A structured, auditable, and business-integrated ISMS that addressed the incidents that triggered the program and positioned Qlyntix to win enterprise contracts, meet client security due diligence requirements, and demonstrate security maturity as a competitive differentiator.

**Frameworks:** ISO/IEC 27001:2022 · ISO/IEC 27002:2022 · AS ISO 31000
**Key deliverables:** ISMS scope document · Risk register · Asset register · SoA (93 controls) · 8-policy suite · Vendor risk framework · Internal audit program · Management review reports · Certification readiness checklist

### 🏗️ OzCloths — Cybersecurity Infrastructure Modernisation

**Context:** OzCloths is a growing Australian online clothing retailer operating on critically exposed legacy infrastructure — open SMB ports exposed to the internet, no VPN for remote workforce access, absent MFA, flat unsegmented networks, unpatched servers, and no incident response capability. The environment was acutely vulnerable to ransomware, credential theft, SQL injection, and DDoS attacks, with no controls in place to detect, contain, or recover from any of them.

**Engagement:** Led a comprehensive security infrastructure modernisation, transforming the legacy environment into a defence-in-depth architecture aligned with NIST CSF and NIST SP 800-53:

- Designed network segmentation architecture with four zones (DMZ, Application, Database, Corporate, Management) and pfSense firewall with strict inbound/outbound rules
- Implemented encrypted WireGuard VPN for all remote workforce access and Snort-based IDS/IPS for real-time threat detection and automated blocking
- Hardened Windows Server, Linux web servers, and NAS storage through OS upgrades, patch management, Fail2Ban, WAF deployment, and MFA enforcement via Duo Security across all privileged entry points
- Implemented RBAC through Active Directory with least-privilege principles across all user functions
- Assessed application layer against OWASP Top 10 — remediating SQL injection, broken access control, cryptographic failures, security misconfiguration, and vulnerable components
- Validated all controls through real attack scenario testing: brute force, SQLi, XSS, DDoS, lateral movement, ransomware backup deletion

**Frameworks:** NIST CSF 2.0 · NIST SP 800-53 Rev 5 · OWASP Top 10
**Key deliverables:** Risk assessment report · Security architecture design · Network segmentation plan · Control implementation report · OWASP Top 10 assessment · Incident response plan · Security test results

### 🤖 NovaMed Health — AI Risk & Governance Assessment *(AI GRC)*

**Context:** NovaMed Health is a fictional Australian private hospital group deploying an AI-assisted clinical decision support tool to generate ranked diagnosis recommendations for clinical staff. The deployment introduced significant risk across patient safety, algorithmic bias, health data privacy, and regulatory compliance under TGA oversight of AI-based medical software — with no governance structure in place to manage the AI system's lifecycle, performance, or failure modes.

**Engagement:** Conducted a structured AI risk and governance assessment using the NIST AI RMF (Govern / Map / Measure / Manage) and ISO/IEC 42001:2023:

- Developed an AI-specific risk register covering clinical model error, algorithmic bias across patient demographics, health data privacy obligations, TGA SaMD classification risk, accountability gaps, and model drift
- Produced a bias and fairness assessment framework with pre-deployment testing requirements across age, gender, and ethnicity cohorts
- Developed an AI Governance Policy establishing accountability structures (AI System Owner, AI Safety and Compliance Committee), model lifecycle controls, human override requirements, and review cadence
- Reviewed Privacy Act 1988 obligations for health information (APPs 3, 5, 6, 11, 12) as applied to AI processing of sensitive patient data
- Produced AI-specific incident response guidance addressing model failures, unexpected outputs, and clinical adverse events linked to AI recommendations
- Conducted ISO/IEC 42001:2023 gap assessment identifying six control areas requiring implementation before deployment

**Frameworks:** NIST AI RMF · ISO/IEC 42001:2023 · Privacy Act 1988 (AU) · TGA SaMD guidance
**Key deliverables:** AI risk register · Bias and fairness assessment · AI governance policy · Privacy impact considerations · AI incident response guidance · ISO 42001 gap assessment

### 🧠 FinSight Analytics — Third-Party AI Procurement GRC Review *(AI GRC)*

**Context:** FinSight Analytics is a fictional APRA-regulated Australian financial services firm evaluating procurement of a third-party AI-driven fraud detection platform from an offshore technology vendor. The procurement carried regulatory obligations under APRA CPS 234, customer protection obligations, and data privacy requirements for customer financial data processed offshore — none of which were addressed in the proposed vendor arrangement.

**Engagement:** Conducted a GRC review of the proposed AI vendor procurement covering risk, compliance, and contractual governance:

- Assessed third-party AI vendor risk across 10 risk dimensions: model accuracy, explainability, data isolation, vendor continuity, breach notification, bias, regulatory change, performance drift, data sovereignty, and governance opacity
- Conducted a gap analysis against APRA CPS 234 third-party information security requirements — identifying six non-compliant areas that would expose FinSight to regulatory risk if the procurement proceeded as structured
- Assessed Privacy Act 1988 APP 8 cross-border disclosure obligations for customer financial data processed by the offshore vendor
- Produced a third-party AI due diligence questionnaire covering model governance, explainability, data handling, security certifications, audit rights, and exit management
- Developed contractual control recommendations covering security standards, breach notification SLAs (24 hours), model performance SLAs, bias disclosure, audit rights, data sovereignty, model change notification, and exit management obligations

**Frameworks:** ISO/IEC 42001:2023 · NIST AI RMF · APRA CPS 234 · Privacy Act 1988 (AU)
**Key deliverables:** Vendor AI risk assessment · APRA CPS 234 gap findings · Third-party AI due diligence questionnaire · Contractual control recommendations · Data sovereignty assessment
